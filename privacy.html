CodX Editor never collects, stores, or accesses your code. All coding happens locally in your browser. However, we use advertising services that may collect browsing data.

1. Overview

At CodX Editor, we are committed to being transparent about how we handle data. This privacy policy explains what we do and don't collect, and how third-party advertising services operate on our platform.

Key Points:

  • Your code never leaves your device - it's processed entirely in your browser
  • We don't collect personal information directly
  • We use Google AdSense, which places cookies and collects data for advertising purposes
  • You can opt out of personalized advertising

2. Google AdSense and Advertising

Our website uses Google AdSense, a third-party advertising service provided by Google LLC. Google AdSense enables us to provide CodX Editor free of charge by displaying advertisements to visitors.

2.1 How Google AdSense Works

Google AdSense uses cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing activity. This information is used to:

  • Display personalized advertisements based on your interests
  • Limit how often you see the same ads
  • Measure ad effectiveness and performance
  • Provide analytics and reporting for advertisers

2.2 Information Collected by Google AdSense

Google AdSense may collect the following types of information:

  • IP address and approximate location
  • Browser type and version
  • Device information (type, operating system, screen size)
  • Pages visited on our website
  • Time and date of your visit
  • Referring website or source
  • Browsing behavior across other websites that use Google advertising services

2.3 The DoubleClick Cookie

Google uses the DoubleClick cookie to enable personalized advertising. This cookie allows Google and its partners to serve ads based on your visits to CodX Editor and other websites across the internet.

Important: While we don't directly collect this data, Google does collect and process it according to their own privacy policy. By using CodX Editor, you consent to Google's data collection and processing as described in this section.

2.4 Opting Out of Personalized Advertising

You have control over personalized advertising. You can opt out through the following methods:

  • Google Ads Settings: Visit https://www.google.com/settings/ads to control personalized ads from Google
  • Industry Opt-Out: Visit https://www.aboutads.info/choices to opt out of multiple advertising networks at once
  • Browser Settings: Configure your browser to block third-party cookies
  • Browser Extensions: Use ad-blocking or privacy-focused browser extensions

For more information about how Google collects and processes data, please review:

  • Google Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Google Advertising: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads

4. How CodX Editor Works

CodX Editor is a client-side application that runs entirely in your web browser.

4.1 Local Storage Only

All your code, projects, and settings are stored in your browser's local storage (localStorage). This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export or share it. You have complete control over this data and can clear it at any time through your browser settings.

4.2 No Server-Side Code Processing

When you write HTML, CSS, or JavaScript in CodX Editor, it's processed and previewed entirely on your device. We don't send your code to any server for processing, compilation, or storage.

4.3 Open Source Libraries

CodX Editor uses open-source libraries for code editing features (such as syntax highlighting). These libraries run locally in your browser and don't transmit data to external servers.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

5.1 Third-Party Cookies

Google AdSense places cookies on your device to enable personalized advertising. These are third-party cookies managed by Google, not by CodX Editor.

5.2 CodX Editor Cookies

CodX Editor itself does not use cookies for tracking or analytics. We only use browser local storage to save your code projects locally on your device.

6. Your Data Rights and Control

6.1 Control Over Your Code

Since all your code is stored locally in your browser:

  • You have complete control over your projects
  • You can export your work at any time
  • You can delete all data by clearing your browser's local storage
  • Your data is not backed up on our servers

6.2 Control Over Advertising Data

For data collected by Google AdSense, you can:

  • Opt out of personalized advertising (see Section 2.4)
  • Clear your browser cookies
  • Use browser privacy features or extensions
  • Review Google's privacy controls at your Google account

7. Children's Privacy

CodX Editor can be used by people of all ages. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children. However, Google AdSense may collect data from all visitors. Parents and educators should be aware that:

  • Children's browsing activity may be tracked by advertising services
  • Personalized ads may be displayed based on browsing behavior
  • Parents can enable privacy controls in Google accounts for children

We recommend that parents review Google's privacy policies and use appropriate parental controls and ad-blocking tools when children use our service.
